However, the article notes that individuals with dyslipidemia or high cardiovascular risk should still monitor their intake.
*   **Avoidance of Trans Fats:** The 8th edition continues to take a hard stance against artificial trans fats, which are significantly detrimental to heart health.
*   **Focus on Whole Foods:** Instead of focusing solely on isolated nutrients, the guidelines advocate for obtaining lipids from high-quality sources like nuts, seeds, fish, and vegetable oils (e.g., olive oil).

### Detailed Overview

The shift in the 8th edition of Taiwan’s DRIs marks a significant departure from older nutritional paradigms that viewed all fats as "bad." The article explains that lipids are vital for cell membrane structure, hormone production, and the absorption of vitamins A, D, E, and K.

The primary goal of the updated lipid guidelines is the prevention of chronic illnesses, particularly Cardiovascular Disease (CVD). By replacing saturated fats with unsaturated fats (MUFAs and PUFAs), individuals can lower their risk profiles significantly.
